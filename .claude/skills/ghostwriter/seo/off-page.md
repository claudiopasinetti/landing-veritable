# Off-Page SEO

**What happens outside your website determines how search engines perceive your authority.**

Off-page SEO is everything you can't control directly on your site: backlinks, brand mentions, social signals, and reputation. It's the web's voting system—where quality and quantity of external signals determine your search rankings.

---

## Backlink Fundamentals

**Backlinks are the currency of search rankings.** Not all links are equal.

### What Makes a Good Backlink

| Factor | Good | Bad |
|--------|------|-----|
| **Authority** | DR/DA 50+, established site | New domain, no traffic, low DR |
| **Relevance** | Same niche/industry | Unrelated topic |
| **Placement** | Editorial content, body text | Footer, sidebar, sitewide |
| **Link Type** | Dofollow (passes authority) | Nofollow (doesn't pass juice) |
| **Anchor Text** | Natural, varied | Over-optimized, spammy |
| **Traffic** | Site gets real visitors | Zero organic traffic |
| **Context** | Surrounded by relevant content | Link farms, PBNs |

### Link Quality Hierarchy

```
Tier 1 (Most Valuable)
├─ Major news sites (NYT, Forbes, BBC)
├─ .edu / .gov domains
├─ Industry authority sites
└─ High-traffic niche blogs

Tier 2 (Valuable)
├─ Medium-sized industry sites
├─ Quality guest posts
├─ Relevant directories (Yelp, industry-specific)
└─ Partner/supplier sites

Tier 3 (Minimal Value)
├─ General directories
├─ Profile links
├─ Forum signatures
└─ Low-traffic blogs

Tier 4 (Avoid)
├─ Link farms
├─ PBNs (private blog networks)
├─ Paid link schemes
└─ Spam directories
```

### Backlink Diversity

Google expects a natural link profile with:
- **Domain diversity**: Links from 100 unique domains > 100 links from 1 domain
- **TLD variety**: Mix of .com, .org, .net, country codes
- **Link type mix**: Editorial, directory, social, forum, etc.
- **Anchor text variety**: Branded, generic, exact match, partial match
- **Temporal distribution**: Gradual growth over time

## Link Building Strategies

### 1. Guest Posting

**What it is**: Writing content for other sites in exchange for backlinks.

**Process**:
1. Find sites accepting guest posts:
   - `"your niche" + "write for us"`
   - `"your niche" + "guest post guidelines"`
   - `"your niche" + "contribute"`
2. Pitch relevant, original topics (not generic)
3. Write high-quality content (1500+ words)
4. Include 1-2 contextual links to your site
5. Build relationship for future posts

**Quality markers**:
- ✅ Editorial review process
- ✅ Site has organic traffic
- ✅ Published authors are real people
- ❌ "Submit guest post" prominently displayed
- ❌ Low-quality existing content
- ❌ Accepts posts from any niche

### 2. Broken Link Building

**What it is**: Finding broken outbound links on authority sites and suggesting your content as replacement.

**Process**:
```bash
# Find broken link opportunities
1. Identify resource pages in your niche
   - "your niche" + "resources"
   - "your niche" + "useful links"

2. Check for broken links using:
   - Ahrefs Site Explorer → Best by Links → Broken backlinks
   - Check My Links (Chrome extension)
   - Screaming Frog (crawl resource pages)

3. Create content that matches/improves the broken link

4. Outreach email:
   Subject: Broken link on [Page Title]

   Hi [Name],

   I was researching [topic] and found your resource page:
   [URL]

   I noticed this link is broken:
   [Broken URL]

   I have a similar resource that might be a good replacement:
   [Your URL]

   Hope this helps!
   [Your name]
```

**Success rate**: 5-15% response rate, 2-5% conversion to links.

### 3. Skyscraper Technique

**What it is**: Find popular content in your niche, create something better, reach out to sites linking to the original.

**Process**:
1. Find link-worthy content:
   - Search your main keyword
   - Analyze top 10 results in Ahrefs/Semrush
   - Sort by referring domains
2. Create 10x better version:
   - More comprehensive
   - Better design/visuals
   - More up-to-date data
   - Better UX (table of contents, visuals)
3. Find sites linking to original:
   - Ahrefs: Enter competitor URL → Backlinks
   - Export link list
4. Outreach to those sites:
   - Mention you found their link to [old content]
   - Your resource is more current/comprehensive
   - Suggest they link to yours

**Best for**: Data studies, ultimate guides, tool lists, comparison posts.

### 4. HARO / Journalist Outreach

**What it is**: Responding to journalist queries for expert sources—earn links from major news sites.

**Platforms**:
- **HARO** (Help A Reporter Out): Free, 3 daily emails with queries
- **Featured**: Paid, curated journalist requests
- **Terkel**: Community-based expert platform
- **SourceBottle**: Australia/UK focused
- **Qwoted**: Journalist-source matching

**How to win**:
```markdown
❌ Bad response:
"I'm an expert in marketing. Happy to help!"

✅ Good response:
Subject: RE: [Exact query topic]

Hi [Journalist name],

I'm [credentials: 10 years in X, worked with Y].

For your piece on [topic]:

1. [Specific insight with data point]
2. [Unique angle they haven't heard]
3. [Quotable soundbite]

I can provide:
- Expert quote
- Original data from [study/clients]
- Case study example

Available for follow-up: [phone] [email]

Best,
[Name + Title + Company]
```

**Response time matters**: Respond within 2 hours of query posting. Journalists work on tight deadlines.

### 5. Resource Page Link Building

**What it is**: Getting listed on curated resource/link pages in your niche.

**Finding resource pages**:
```
Search queries:
- "your niche" + "resources"
- "your niche" + "useful tools"
- "your niche" + "links"
- inurl:links + "your niche"
- intitle:resources + "your niche"
```

**Pitch template**:
```
Subject: Resource suggestion for [Page Title]

Hi [Name],

I found your resource page on [topic]: [URL]

I noticed you link to [example resource they list].

I recently created [describe your resource]:
[Your URL]

It covers:
- [Unique value point 1]
- [Unique value point 2]
- [Unique value point 3]

Would this be a good fit for your page?

Thanks,
[Name]
```

**What makes your resource link-worthy**:
- Free tool/calculator
- Original research/data
- Comprehensive guide
- Visual assets (infographic, templates)

### 6. Digital PR

**What it is**: Creating newsworthy content that earns media coverage and links.

**Link-worthy assets**:
- **Data studies**: Survey your audience, publish findings
- **Industry reports**: Annual state of X reports
- **Original research**: New data/insights
- **Reactive content**: Comment on trending news in your niche
- **Newsjacking**: Tie your expertise to breaking news
- **Stunts/campaigns**: Creative, share-worthy projects

**Example**: "We analyzed 10,000 [X] and found [surprising insight]"

**Distribution**:
1. Create press release
2. Pitch to niche industry blogs
3. Pitch to journalists covering your space
4. Share in relevant communities
5. Paid amplification (if budget allows)

## Anchor Text Optimization

**Anchor text is the clickable text in a hyperlink.** Google uses it to understand what your page is about.

### Anchor Text Types

| Type | Example | When to Use |
|------|---------|-------------|
| **Branded** | "Acme Corp" | Most common—safe |
| **Naked URL** | "acmecorp.com" | Natural diversity |
| **Exact Match** | "blue widgets" | Sparingly—risky if overused |
| **Partial Match** | "best blue widgets" | Moderate use |
| **Generic** | "click here", "this post" | Some natural links |
| **Image** | (alt text) | When image is link |
| **Long-tail** | "how to buy blue widgets online" | Occasional |

### Ideal Anchor Text Distribution

```
Natural Link Profile (Recommended):
├─ Branded: 40-50%
├─ Naked URL: 15-20%
├─ Generic: 15-20%
├─ Partial Match: 10-15%
├─ Exact Match: 5-10%
└─ Long-tail: 2-5%

Over-Optimized (Risky):
├─ Exact Match: 60%+ ⚠️ PENALTY RISK
├─ Branded: 10%
└─ Generic: 30%
```

### Anchor Text Best Practices

✅ **Do**:
- Vary anchor text naturally
- Use branded anchors most often
- Let others choose anchor text when possible
- Use descriptive anchors (not "click here")
- Match anchor to target page topic

❌ **Don't**:
- Overuse exact match keywords (triggers Penguin penalty)
- Use same anchor text repeatedly
- Force unnatural keyword-stuffed anchors
- Optimize anchors in footer/sidebar links
- Use generic anchors for important pages

## Domain Authority / Domain Rating

**Domain Authority (DA)** is a score (0-100) predicting how well a site will rank. Developed by Moz, not a Google metric.

### How It's Calculated

**Factors**:
- Number of linking domains
- Quality of linking domains
- Link diversity
- Content quality signals
- Age of domain
- Historical ranking performance

**Tools & Their Metrics**:
- **Moz**: Domain Authority (DA)
- **Ahrefs**: Domain Rating (DR)
- **Semrush**: Authority Score (AS)
- **Majestic**: Trust Flow (TF) / Citation Flow (CF)

### Score Interpretation

| Score | Meaning | Example |
|-------|---------|---------|
| 0-20 | New/weak site | Personal blogs, new startups |
| 20-40 | Growing authority | Established small business |
| 40-60 | Strong authority | Industry blogs, medium sites |
| 60-80 | High authority | Major brands, large publishers |
| 80-100 | Dominant authority | Google, Amazon, NYT, Wikipedia |

### How to Improve Domain Authority

**Short answer**: Get more high-quality backlinks from authoritative domains.

**Strategies**:
1. **Link building** (see strategies above)
2. **Remove toxic links** (see disavow section)
3. **Create linkable assets** (tools, studies, guides)
4. **Improve existing content** (update old posts)
5. **Fix technical SEO issues** (crawlability, speed)
6. **Build relationships** (network with other sites)

**Timeline**: DA/DR moves slowly. Expect 3-6 months to see significant change.

**Important**: Don't obsess over DA/DR—focus on **relevance** and **traffic** from links, not just metrics.

## Toxic Links and Disavow

**Toxic links hurt your rankings.** Google's Penguin algorithm penalizes sites with spammy link profiles.

### Identifying Toxic Links

**Red flags**:
- ❌ Link farms / PBNs
- ❌ Sites with no organic traffic
- ❌ Unrelated niches (pharma, gambling, adult)
- ❌ Foreign language sites (if you're English-only)
- ❌ Over-optimized anchor text (all exact match)
- ❌ Sitewide links from low-quality sites
- ❌ Links from penalized domains

**Tools for finding toxic links**:
```
Google Search Console → Links → Top linking sites
  ↓
Export link data
  ↓
Analyze in:
- Ahrefs Site Explorer → Backlinks
- Semrush Backlink Audit
- Moz Link Explorer
  ↓
Filter for:
- Low DR/DA (under 10)
- Spam score (over 60%)
- Irrelevant topics
- Link velocity spikes
```

### When to Disavow

**Use Google Disavow Tool only if**:
1. You've received a manual penalty (Google Search Console notification)
2. You have obvious toxic links from SEO attacks (negative SEO)
3. You bought links in the past and want to clean up

**Don't disavow if**:
- You have a few random low-quality links (normal)
- You're trying to pre-emptively avoid penalties
- You haven't tried manual removal first

### Disavow Process

**Step 1**: Try manual removal
- Find contact info for linking site
- Request link removal (email template below)
- Document attempts (save emails)

**Manual removal email**:
```
Subject: Link removal request

Hi,

I noticed you link to my site from:
[Their URL]

Linking to:
[Your URL]

Could you please remove this link? We're cleaning up our backlink profile.

Thank you,
[Name]
```

**Step 2**: Wait 2-4 weeks for responses

**Step 3**: Create disavow file for non-responders
```
# Links to disavow
# Last updated: 2026-02-02

# Spam directory
domain:spammy-directory.com

# Low quality blog
domain:low-quality-blog.net

# Specific toxic page
https://another-site.com/spam-page

# Block entire domain (most common)
domain:another-spam-site.com
```

**Step 4**: Upload to Google Disavow Tool
- Go to: https://search.google.com/search-console/disavow-links
- Select property
- Upload .txt file

**Warning**: Disavowing legitimate links can hurt rankings. Be conservative.

## Brand Mentions and Unlinked Mentions

**Brand mentions without links still signal authority to Google.** But linked mentions are better.

### Finding Unlinked Mentions

**Tools**:
```
Google Alerts:
- Set up alert for "Your Brand Name"
- Receive emails when mentioned

Ahrefs Content Explorer:
- Search: "your brand" -site:yourdomain.com
- Filter: Not linked to your domain

Brand24 / Mention:
- Real-time brand monitoring
- Social + web mentions

Google Search:
- "your brand name" -site:yourdomain.com
- Check for unlinked mentions manually
```

### Converting Mentions to Links

**Outreach email**:
```
Subject: Quick request - article mentioning [Your Brand]

Hi [Author name],

I saw your article "[Article Title]" and loved how you mentioned [Your Brand]!

[Specific compliment about their content]

Would you be open to linking to our site when you mention us?
[Your URL]

It would help readers find more info.

Thanks!
[Your Name]
[Your Title]
```

**Conversion rate**: 20-40% if the mention is already positive.

**Pro tip**: Offer value in return (share their article, provide expert quote for future pieces, offer free trial of your product).

## Local SEO Off-Page

**Local businesses need local signals** to rank in map pack and local results.

### Google Business Profile (GBP)

**Optimization checklist**:
- ✅ Claim and verify listing
- ✅ Complete all sections (100% profile)
- ✅ Choose correct primary category
- ✅ Add 5-10 secondary categories
- ✅ Write keyword-rich description (750 chars)
- ✅ Add business hours (including holidays)
- ✅ Upload 10+ high-quality photos
- ✅ Add products/services
- ✅ Enable messaging
- ✅ Post weekly updates (Google Posts)
- ✅ Respond to ALL reviews (within 24 hours)

**Google Posts**: Publish 1-2x per week
- Event announcements
- Special offers
- New products/services
- Blog post summaries

### Citations

**Citation = mention of your business NAP** (Name, Address, Phone) on other websites.

**Tier 1 citations (must-haves)**:
- Google Business Profile
- Bing Places
- Apple Maps
- Yelp
- Facebook
- BBB (Better Business Bureau)

**Tier 2 citations (important)**:
- YellowPages
- Foursquare
- MapQuest
- Yahoo Local
- Angi (formerly Angie's List)
- Industry-specific directories

**Tier 3 citations (nice-to-have)**:
- Local chamber of commerce
- Local news sites
- Local blogs
- Niche directories (lawyer, restaurant, etc.)

### NAP Consistency

**Critical**: Your business info must be **identical** across all citations.

```
✅ Correct (consistent):
Acme Marketing LLC
123 Main Street, Suite 200
Chicago, IL 60601
(312) 555-1234

❌ Wrong (inconsistent):
Acme Marketing                    [Missing LLC]
123 Main St. Ste 200              [Abbreviated]
Chicago Illinois 60601            [No state abbreviation]
312-555-1234                      [Different phone format]
```

**Inconsistencies confuse Google** → lower local rankings.

**Tools to check NAP**:
- Moz Local
- BrightLocal
- Whitespark Local Citation Finder

### Local Link Building

**Strategies**:
- Sponsor local events (get link from event page)
- Partner with local businesses (link exchanges)
- Join chamber of commerce (directory link)
- Get featured in local news (media coverage)
- Sponsor local sports teams/schools
- Create local resource (city guides, local statistics)

### Reviews

**Reviews are a top 3 local ranking factor.**

**Review strategy**:
1. Ask every satisfied customer for review
2. Make it easy (send direct review link)
3. Respond to all reviews (positive and negative)
4. Aim for 4.0+ star average
5. Get 5-10 new reviews per month (steady velocity)

**Review platforms**:
- Google (most important for SEO)
- Yelp
- Facebook
- Industry-specific (TripAdvisor, Healthgrades, etc.)

**Responding to negative reviews**:
```
❌ Bad response:
"You're wrong. Our service is great."

✅ Good response:
"Thank you for your feedback. I'm sorry we didn't meet your expectations.
I'd like to make this right—please contact me directly at [email] so we
can resolve this. - [Manager Name]"
```

## Competitor Backlink Analysis

**Your competitors' backlinks are your link building roadmap.**

### Process

**Step 1**: Identify competitors
```
Google search: [your main keywords]
  ↓
Top 5-10 ranking sites
  ↓
Analyze their backlink profiles
```

**Step 2**: Export backlink data
```
Ahrefs:
Enter competitor domain → Backlinks → Export

Semrush:
Backlink Analytics → Enter domain → Backlinks → Export

Moz:
Link Explorer → Enter domain → Inbound Links → Export
```

**Step 3**: Analyze patterns

**Questions to ask**:
- What types of sites link to them?
- What content formats earn links? (guides, tools, data)
- What anchor text do they use?
- What's their link velocity (growth rate)?
- Are they doing guest posting? Where?
- What directories are they listed in?
- Who links to multiple competitors but not you?

**Step 4**: Find link gap opportunities
```
Ahrefs:
Link Intersect → Enter 3 competitor domains + your domain
  ↓
Shows: Sites linking to them but not you
  ↓
Outreach targets
```

### What to Look For

**Link sources**:
| Competitor Has | Your Action |
|----------------|-------------|
| Guest posts on Site X | Pitch Site X |
| Listed in Directory Y | Submit to Directory Y |
| Links from Resource Page Z | Request inclusion on Page Z |
| Data study with 500 links | Create better data study |
| Free tool with 200 links | Build competing/better tool |

**Quality vs. quantity**:
- Competitor with 1,000 low-quality links → Don't copy
- Competitor with 50 high-authority links → Study their strategy

## Link Velocity

**Link velocity = rate at which you acquire backlinks over time.**

### Natural Growth Patterns

```
New site (0-6 months):
├─ 0-5 links per month
└─ Slow, steady growth

Established site (6-24 months):
├─ 5-20 links per month
└─ Consistent growth

Authority site (24+ months):
├─ 20-100+ links per month
└─ Natural viral spikes okay
```

### Red Flags (Unnatural Patterns)

❌ **Sudden spike**: 0 links → 500 links in one week
- **Cause**: Bought links, link scheme, PBN
- **Risk**: Penguin penalty

❌ **Consistent flat rate**: Exactly 50 links per month every month
- **Cause**: Automated link building
- **Risk**: Looks manipulative

❌ **All links same date**: 200 links all created Jan 1
- **Cause**: Bulk submission to directories
- **Risk**: Unnatural pattern

✅ **Natural pattern**: Gradual growth with occasional spikes
- **Cause**: Viral content, PR, steady outreach
- **Risk**: None—looks organic

### Safe Link Acquisition Rate

**Guidelines by domain age**:

| Domain Age | Safe Monthly Links | Notes |
|------------|-------------------|-------|
| 0-3 months | 3-10 links | Slow start, focus on quality |
| 3-6 months | 10-20 links | Gradual increase |
| 6-12 months | 20-50 links | Growth phase |
| 12-24 months | 50-100 links | Established site |
| 24+ months | 100+ links | Authority site, viral okay |

**Important**: These are averages. Natural sites have variation—some months 5 links, some months 50.

### Velocity Spikes That Are Safe

✅ **Viral content**: One piece gets 500 links in a week
✅ **Media coverage**: Press release → 100 news sites link
✅ **Launch announcement**: New product → industry coverage
✅ **Event sponsorship**: 50 event sites link at once

**Key**: Spikes are fine if they're **organic and explainable**.

## E-E-A-T Off-Page Signals

**E-E-A-T = Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness**

Google's quality raters use E-E-A-T to evaluate content. Off-page signals prove your expertise to Google.

### Off-Page E-E-A-T Signals

**Experience**:
- First-hand testimonials from customers
- Case studies published on third-party sites
- Reviews mentioning specific experiences with your brand
- Social proof (awards, recognitions)

**Expertise**:
- Author bylines on major publications
- Speaking engagements (conference sites link to you)
- Certifications and credentials (linked from issuing organizations)
- Academic publications (Google Scholar profile)
- Industry association memberships

**Authoritativeness**:
- Links from authoritative sites in your niche
- Brand mentions in industry reports
- Citations in research papers
- Wikipedia page (if notable enough)
- Press coverage from major outlets

**Trustworthiness**:
- Better Business Bureau accreditation
- Trust seals (Norton, McAfee)
- Third-party reviews (Trustpilot, G2, Capterra)
- No toxic backlinks or spammy associations
- Consistent NAP across the web

### Building Author Authority

**For content creators / thought leaders**:

**Tactics**:
1. **Publish on authority sites**: Forbes, HuffPost, industry blogs
2. **Maintain social profiles**: Twitter, LinkedIn with expertise signals
3. **Get bylines**: Ensure author bio + link on all guest posts
4. **Speak at events**: Conference sites link to speaker profiles
5. **Create Wikipedia page**: If you meet notability guidelines
6. **Build personal brand**: Consistent name, photo, bio across platforms
7. **Earn industry awards**: Awards = third-party validation

**Schema markup for authors**:

### Building Brand Trust

**For businesses**:

**Tactics**:
1. **Claim review profiles**: Google, Yelp, Trustpilot, G2
2. **Respond to reviews**: Shows you care (trust signal)
3. **Get press coverage**: Media links = trust
4. **Display trust badges**: SSL, security, privacy certifications
5. **Be transparent**: About page, team photos, contact info
6. **Show social proof**: Customer logos, testimonials, case studies
7. **Avoid penalties**: No spammy links, no black hat tactics

**Trust-killing off-page signals**:
- ❌ Links from spam sites
- ❌ Negative reviews without responses
- ❌ BBB complaints unresolved
- ❌ Scam reports on RipoffReport
- ❌ No social media presence (looks fake)

## Off-Page SEO Checklist

### Monthly Tasks

- [ ] Monitor new backlinks (Google Search Console)
